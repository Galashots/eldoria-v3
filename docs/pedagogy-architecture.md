# Eldoria-V3 Pedagogical Architecture and Curriculum Integration Report

## 1. Executive Summary
The conceptualization and ongoing development of the Eldoria-V2 learning modules represent a highly sophisticated intersection of modern 2D role-playing game (RPG) architecture and evidence-based pedagogical theory. The core mandate of this initiative requires the synthesis of a beautiful, immersive gaming experience with the rigorous curricular standards of the newly updated Alberta Kindergarten to Grade 6 (K-6) framework, specifically targeting Grade 2 and Grade 5 learning outcomes. Furthermore, the architectural parameters demand that the digital environment directly reflects the specific instructional philosophies and cultural expectations of the Calgary Board of Education (CBE) Traditional Learning Centre (TLC) program, modeled explicitly after the academic ecosystem of Glamorgan School. [1][2][3][4][5]

This comprehensive reference guide establishes a deterministic blueprint for integrating the latest Alberta curriculum updates into the game's core loops. It provides exhaustive guidance on the deployment of stealth assessment mechanics, dictating precise parameters for question types, difficulty scaling, and systemic structure. Crucially, the report delineates the translation of Glamorgan School's unique TLC principles—including Character Education, the "Grizzly Leaders" ethos, structured literacy paradigms driven by the CBE Reading Decision Tree, and the Gradual Release of Responsibility (GRR) instructional model—into playable, curriculum-aligned mechanics. By adhering to the structural and pedagogical frameworks detailed herein, Eldoria-V3 will function not merely as a supplementary "edutainment" product, but as a fully integrated extension of the Glamorgan TLC classroom environment. [1][2][3][4][5]

## 2. The Glamorgan School Context and the TLC Paradigm
To achieve efficacy as a learning tool for students attending Glamorgan School, Eldoria-V2 must culturally, mechanically, and philosophically mirror the Traditional Learning Centre's foundational pillars. The TLC model subverts contemporary, hyper-flexible progressive education trends in favor of teacher-directed instruction, a highly structured virtues-oriented environment, explicit foundational skill-building, and high expectations for both academic achievement and personal conduct. Translating these methodologies into a 2D RPG requires a careful reimagining of standard gaming tropes, replacing arbitrary power fantasies with systems that reward ethical citizenship and disciplined academic mastery. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 2.1 Character Education and the Virtues System
At the nucleus of the TLC philosophy is Character Education, which at Glamorgan School is deeply intertwined with "The Leader in Me" program and the "7 Habits of Highly Effective People". The three central pillars of the TLC program—Respect for Self, Respect for Others, and Respect for the Environment—must replace traditional RPG alignment systems (such as the standard Good versus Evil dichotomy). In Eldoria-V3, character progression and narrative branching must be intrinsically tied to the demonstration of these virtues. [1][2][3][4][5]

The application of the 7 Habits provides a mechanical framework for quest design. Habit 1, "Be Proactive," translates into environmental mechanics where players are rewarded for repairing town infrastructure or gathering resources before a scripted environmental crisis occurs, thereby demonstrating foresight. Habit 4, "Think Win-Win," necessitates complex dialogue trees that require the player to negotiate between two conflicting non-player characters (NPCs) to find a mutually beneficial solution, rather than violently eliminating one faction. Habit 5, "Seek First to Understand, Then to be Understood," dictates investigation mechanics wherein the player must collect clues and passively listen to multiple NPC perspectives before the dialogue option required to resolve a conflict becomes available to the player. [1][2][3][4][5]

Glamorgan School's motto, "To Think, to Value, To Know," alongside its mascot, the Grizzly (often operationalized as the "Grizzly Leaders" student recognition program), provides a powerful thematic anchor for the game's lore. Eldoria-V3 should incorporate a "Grizzly Vanguard" or similar elite faction that serves as the moral and academic compass of the game world. Progression within this faction rewards the player with cosmetic armor layers. To further reinforce the TLC environment, these cosmetic rewards should include green and navy tartans, directly reflecting the mandatory formal uniform policies maintained by Glamorgan School and other CBE TLC institutions. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 2.2 The Gradual Release of Responsibility in Level Design
The primary instructional methodology deployed across TLC schools is the Gradual Release of Responsibility (GRR) model, traditionally summarized as "I do it, We do it, You do it together, You do it alone". Eldoria-V3 must structure its onboarding sequences, mechanical tutorials, and cognitive scaffolding to mimic this exact progression. Level design must systematically transition the player from passive observation to independent mastery. [1][2][3][4][5]

The "I do it" phase requires an NPC or a scripted environmental sequence to clearly demonstrate a new mechanic, such as utilizing a lever to alter the flow of a river. The "We do it" phase immediately follows, allowing the player to perform the action with robust, non-punitive user interface (UI) prompts and continuous NPC verbal guidance. The "You do it together" phase places the player in a low-stakes puzzle environment alongside an AI companion character that provides contextual hints only if the player's telemetry indicates a prolonged stall. Finally, the "You do it alone" phase serves as the summative stealth assessment; the player faces a dungeon puzzle or combat scenario where independent mastery of the mechanic is absolutely required to progress, with the game engine logging the success rate and time-to-completion. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 2.3 Structured Literacy and Disciplinary Comprehension
TLC programs differentiate themselves through an uncompromising commitment to early structured literacy, emphasizing explicit, direct instruction in phonics, spelling, graphemes, and grammar. Furthermore, the most recent Glamorgan School Development Plan (SDP) highlights a critical, school-wide focus on improving disciplinary literacy, reading comprehension, and the utilization of the CBE Reading Decision Tree. The SDP notes that while reading comprehension is improving, there is a distinct need to strengthen instruction in inference, synthesis, and the mastery of domain-specific academic vocabulary. [1][2][3][4][5]

To support this mandate, Eldoria-V3's text rendering and dialogue systems must feature dynamic, age-differentiated profiles. A specifically designed "Younger-Reader Profile" for Grade 2 students must utilize morphological highlighting—visually distinguishing specific digraphs, blends, and vowels within the dialogue box—while providing optional read-aloud functionality. Conversely, the "Older-Reader Profile" for Grade 5 students must introduce complex syntax and demand higher-order inferential thinking. Vocabulary utilized in early-game quests must intentionally integrate Tier 2 (high-utility) and Tier 3 (domain-specific) words, thereby exposing students to rigorous academic language within a compelling narrative context, perfectly aligning with the vocabulary development goals outlined in the CBE Reading Decision Tree interventions. [1][2][3][4][5]

## 3. Architectural Guidance on Question Type, Difficulty, and Structure
The traditional educational software paradigm relies on overt, disruptive assessment, frequently pausing the interactive experience to present the user with a multiple-choice question. This approach breaks narrative immersion and often triggers test anxiety, actively working against the psychological state of "flow" required for deep learning. Eldoria-V3 must strictly adhere to the Evidence-Centered Design (ECD) framework for stealth assessment, ensuring that evidence of learning is gathered seamlessly through gameplay logs, environmental choices, and crafted outputs. The structure, typology, and scaling of these assessments must be meticulously engineered to support the Alberta curriculum without compromising the integrity of the RPG experience. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 3.1 Typology of Stealth Assessment Questions
Stealth assessment in Eldoria-V3 relies on translating curriculum Knowledge, Understanding, Skills, and Procedures (KUSPs) into diegetic game actions. The "questions" are not formatted as text prompts, but as environmental or social obstacles that require the application of specific academic skills to overcome. [1][2][3][4][5]

The first primary question type is the Action-Based Assessment. In this structure, the player's physical interaction with the game's physics or crafting engine serves as the answer. For instance, if a Grade 2 student is tasked with repairing a collapsed bridge, they are not presented with a text box asking, "Which geometric shape provides the greatest structural integrity?" Instead, they are provided with an inventory of two-dimensional shapes (triangles, squares, rectangles) and must physically construct the bridge using the game's building interface. The telemetry system silently logs their initial selection, the number of structural failures experienced, and the time taken to adopt and rely upon the triangular truss design. [1][2][3][4][5]

The second primary type is the Resource Allocation Assessment, utilized primarily for mathematical reasoning and financial literacy. This structure places the player in scenarios governed by scarcity. If a player possesses a limited amount of in-game currency and must provision a caravan for a journey across a desert, the game logs their budgeting efficiency. The assessment tracks whether the player prioritized essential needs (water, rations) over superficial wants (cosmetic armor upgrades), directly linking gameplay behavior to the Grade 2 financial literacy outcomes regarding the differentiation of needs and wants. [1][2][3][4][5]

The third primary type is the Dialogue-Based Assessment, designed to evaluate reading comprehension, historical empathy, and social studies competencies. NPCs will present complex socio-political scenarios to the player. In a Grade 5 context, a player might mediate a debate between two factions regarding individual rights versus collective security. The dialogue choices the player selects do not simply advance the plot; they are logged as evidence of the player's understanding of the underlying ideologies, allowing the game to assess comprehension of democratic principles without breaking immersion. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 3.2 Difficulty Scaling and Cognitive Load Management
Difficulty scaling in traditional RPGs typically relies on statistical inflation—increasing enemy health pools or damage output—which invariably encourages mindless, repetitive grinding. This mechanical padding actively contravenes the design principles required for educational efficacy. In Eldoria-V3, difficulty must scale exclusively through cognitive complexity, variable introduction, and the fading of instructional scaffolding. [1][2][3][4][5]

The core scaling mechanism is Scaffolded Micro-Mastery, an extension of the TLC's Gradual Release of Responsibility. A mechanic is initially introduced with a single isolated variable. In a mathematical context, the player might first be required to calculate the perimeter of a simple, unobstructed rectangular farm plot to determine how much fencing is required. Once mastery is logged, the difficulty scales by introducing a second variable, such as an irregular polygon shape caused by a rocky outcropping on the farm. The final tier of difficulty introduces temporal pressure, requiring the player to rapidly calculate the perimeter and construct the fence before a scripted event, such as a monster incursion, breaches the boundary. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 3.3 Non-Punitive Feedback Structures
A critical component of the game's pedagogical architecture is the implementation of non-punitive failure states. If a student fails an embedded assessment (e.g., miscalculating an algebraic expression required to unlock a door, or failing to balance a budget), the game must not halt progress with a traditional "Game Over" screen. Such hard failure states breed frustration and disengagement. Instead, failure must alter the narrative path slightly or trigger adaptive scaffolding. An incorrect calculation might cause the door mechanism to spark and temporarily jam, prompting an NPC companion to offer a contextual hint or a simplified version of the problem, ensuring the feedback loop helps the player recover from mistakes while still logging the initial error for the parent/teacher dashboard. [1][2][3][4][5]

The structural integration of these question types, scaling methodologies, and feedback loops ensures that the assessment mechanism remains entirely invisible to the player. The student experiences a cohesive, challenging fantasy adventure, while the backend database constructs a highly granular, real-time portrait of their academic proficiencies and areas requiring further TLC-aligned intervention. [1][2][3][4][5]

## 4. Grade 2 Curriculum Translation to Gameplay Modules
The Grade 2 Alberta curriculum introduces foundational concepts that require concrete, highly visual, and interactive representations. Children in the 7-to-8-year-old cognitive band benefit from explicit objectives, clear auditory cues, and immediate, tangible feedback within their learning environments. The translation of these specific KUSPs into Eldoria-V2 mechanics forms the baseline of the game's early narrative arcs. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 4.1 Mathematics: Number Sense, Operations, and Financial Literacy
The Grade 2 mathematics curriculum demands that students develop a robust understanding of natural numbers up to 1,000, including the ability to identify digits representing thousands, hundreds, tens, and ones based on place value. Students must master skip counting by 20s, 25s, and 50s, and apply addition and subtraction strategies for numbers up to 100. Furthermore, financial literacy is heavily integrated, requiring students to count money, estimate values, and express monetary values in both cents and dollars using decimal notation. The curriculum also introduces foundational fraction concepts, specifically modeling unit fractions limited to denominators of 10 or less. [1][2][3][4][5]

These mathematical requirements are seamlessly integrated into the game's core economic and crafting systems. The in-game economy of the starting village, Sunmere, utilizes a base-10 currency system (e.g., Copper, Silver, Gold) that explicitly mirrors the Canadian decimal system. When purchasing items from a vendor, the UI avoids simple "Buy" buttons; instead, it requires the player to manually construct the payment using a drag-and-drop coin interface, assessing their ability to skip count and decompose quantities into 100s, 10s, and 1s. Fractional understanding is assessed through a recurring resource-sharing mini-game, where the player must divide gathered materials (such as a loaf of bread or a bundle of wood) among a specific number of NPCs, effectively modeling a unit fraction by partitioning a whole object into equal parts. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 4.2 Science: Matter, Energy, and Computer Science
The newly updated Alberta Science curriculum heavily emphasizes active investigation and introduces formal computer science concepts at the early elementary level. Grade 2 learning outcomes focus on the physical properties of matter, the behaviors of light and sound, Earth's landforms and bodies of water, and the application of creativity to design precise, reliable, and efficient algorithmic instructions. [1][2][3][4][5]

The computer science requirement is addressed through a recurring "Golem Programming" mechanic. To access deeper dungeon levels, the player must control a broken automaton. Instead of direct joystick control, the player must place directional command blocks (Up, Down, Interact, Repeat) into a logical sequence to navigate the golem through a maze. If the golem crashes, the player engages in the formal process of "debugging" the sequence, generating highly specific stealth assessment data on early computational thinking. Energy concepts are translated into environmental puzzles. Light puzzles require the player to redirect sunbeams through dark caverns using rotatable mirrors, explicitly assessing their understanding of how light travels in straight lines and reflects off surfaces. Sound mechanics involve interacting with different materials (e.g., striking a metallic bell versus a wooden block) to observe variations in vibration and absorption, unlocking pathways based on acoustic properties. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 4.3 Social Studies: Communities, Time, and Place
The finalized 2025/2026 Social Studies curriculum for Grade 2 is structured around exploring Canada's diverse land and cultures, understanding systems of needs and wants, and developing foundational citizenship and decision-making skills. The curriculum requires students to examine the connections between natural resources, employment, and trade, while understanding how communities meet their collective needs. [1][2][3][4][5]

These outcomes are woven directly into Eldoria-V3's overarching meta-loop: the restoration of a damaged village. As players gather resources, they must determine how to allocate them. Quests will explicitly force the player to differentiate between what the town needs for survival (e.g., repairing a well for clean water) versus what it merely wants (e.g., constructing a decorative plaza). When resources are scarce, the player must gather the town council and facilitate a vote, exposing the student to democratic processes, formal and informal civic participation, and the challenges of group cooperation. [1][2][3][4][5]

### 4.4 English Language Arts and Literature (ELAL)
The Grade 2 ELAL framework focuses on solidifying phonics, understanding morphemes to expand vocabulary, and utilizing a variety of reading strategies (such as altering speed, rereading, and making predictions) to comprehend texts. The curriculum emphasizes the integration of Tier 2 (high-utility) words and the ability to combine ideas in logical sequences. [1][2][3][4][5]

In alignment with Glamorgan's structured literacy approach, the game's dialogue engine highlights critical graphemes and morphemes. Narrative quests require students to logically sequence events; for example, a quest might involve finding scattered pages of a town chronicle and requiring the player to order the events chronologically before the lore can be accessed. Vocabulary acquisition is tracked as players encounter and utilize specific Tier 2 words required to unlock advanced dialogue options. [1][2][3][4][5]

## 5. Grade 5 Curriculum Translation to Gameplay Modules
By Grade 5, students (typically ages 10 to 11) possess the cognitive capacity to manage complex, intersecting systems, engage in abstract mathematical reasoning, and navigate nuanced moral ambiguity. Correspondingly, Eldoria-V3's presentation shifts to the "Older-Reader Profile." This shift significantly reduces overt instructional hand-holding, introduces denser text, deploys complex UI management panels, and requires the synthesis of multiple data points to achieve multi-step objectives. The gameplay evolves from localized problem-solving to macro-level systemic management. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

### 5.1 Mathematics: Complex Operations, Fractions, and Coordinate Geometry
The Grade 5 mathematics curriculum represents a steep escalation in cognitive load. Students are required to handle numbers up to 1,000,000, execute standard algorithms for addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, and fluidly convert between improper fractions and mixed numbers. The curriculum also formally introduces algebraic expressions, the evaluation of numerical expressions using the order of operations, and navigation using the coordinate grid. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

These advanced concepts are woven deeply into the game's navigation and crafting systems. The world map and dungeon mini-maps transition from simple, localized waypoints to a rigid Cartesian coordinate system. Treasure maps discovered as loot no longer display a glowing, omniscient marker; instead, they provide a coordinate pair (X,Y) alongside an algebraic expression that the player must correctly evaluate to determine the exact dig site. Advanced armor crafting requires the player to mix materials in precise fractional proportions. For example, forging a "Steel Breastplate" might require an input of 13/4 units of raw iron. The player must mentally convert this improper fraction into a mixed number (3 1/4) to manually select the correct number of full ingots and quarter-ingots from their inventory interface. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

### 5.2 Science: Particle Model, Forces of Flight, and Biological Systems
Grade 5 Science delves deeply into the physical properties of matter via the particle model, the physics of flight (specifically lift, drag, weight, and thrust) in water and air, renewable and non-renewable energy resources, and the functions of internal biological systems (digestive, respiratory, circulatory). Furthermore, students must analyze the relationships among climate, weather, and agricultural practices. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

Aerodynamics and fluid dynamics are integrated into the game's advanced traversal mechanics. To navigate expansive wilderness zones, the player unlocks a glider apparatus. The physics engine governing this traversal explicitly models thrust, lift, drag, and weight. To cross a wide chasm, the player must manually adjust the surface area of their glider (directly affecting lift and drag) based on the current randomized wind speed (weather systems) to achieve stable flight, generating stealth assessment data on their understanding of opposing forces. Environmental puzzles require the manipulation of the states of matter based on the particle model. Applying a "Heat" spell to an ice barricade increases the movement of the particles, breaking the strong attractive forces of the solid and initiating a phase change to a liquid, which then flows into a mechanism to open a door. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

### 5.3 Social Studies: Ancient Civilizations and Democracy
The mandatory 2026-27 implementation of the Grade 5 Social Studies curriculum focuses extensively on the rise, influence, and fall of ancient civilizations, emphasizing how environmental factors shaped these societies. Crucially, it explores the development of diverse democratic principles, requiring students to compare Athenian direct democracy, the Roman Republic's representative democracy, and the consensus-based decision-making of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy. The curriculum also covers the foundations of Canadian history, including Confederation, the Charter of Rights and Freedoms, and the distinction between democratic and totalitarian systems.

These historical and civic concepts form the backbone of Eldoria-V3's late-game lore and factional conflict. As the player explores ancient ruins, they discover codex entries, artifacts, and murals that directly encode the legacies of these historical models. A major questline involves recovering the "Lost Charter," requiring the player to piece together fragmented laws based on the Haudenosaunee Great Law of Peace. The player must then mediate a dispute between two town factions, applying these consensus-based principles to find a resolution, thereby assessing their understanding of cooperative governance versus totalitarian dictates. The town meta-loop upgrades to include complex economic management, forcing the player to set tax rates to fund town defenses, thereby teaching the concepts of production, consumption, surplus, deficit, and debt. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

### 5.4 English Language Arts and Literature (ELAL)
In Grade 5, ELAL expectations shift toward engaging in collaborative dialogue to solve problems, applying advanced vocabulary to complex texts, and analyzing how text genres and structures support precise communication. Students are expected to evaluate ideas, identify bias, and synthesize information from multiple sources. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

Aligned with the Glamorgan SDP's focus on reading comprehension and disciplinary literacy, Grade 5 quests in Eldoria-V2 are highly text-dependent. Players must read and synthesize contradictory reports from different NPCs to uncover a spy within the village. The game tracks how thoroughly the player investigates dialogue trees and whether they successfully identify logical inconsistencies in the text, providing robust data on their ability to evaluate information and comprehend complex narratives. [1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

## 6. Technical Ecosystem Integration for Data and Telemetry
The successful execution of this highly complex, data-driven RPG necessitates a robust, cross-platform backend. The integration of the Google Suite into the existing Antigravity IDE, Codex, and Claude workflows is vital for maintaining deterministic asset pipelines and handling the massive influx of stealth assessment telemetry.

### 6.1 Metadata Generation and Generative AI
Eldoria-V3 requires a strict, machine-readable asset pipeline prioritizing Aseprite source files exported to PNG with JSON metadata. Gemini 1.5 Pro serves as the primary technical metadata generator within this workflow. Gemini can ingest raw animation frame counts and collision data parameters and instantly output perfectly formatted JSON files defining hitbox_player, hurtbox, and interact_trigger layers for each frame of animation. Furthermore, Gemini can ingest a master narrative script and automatically generate two distinct JSON dialogue trees: one utilizing Tier 2 vocabulary and morphological highlighting suited for the Grade 2 "Younger-Reader Profile," and another utilizing complex syntax for the Grade 5 "Older-Reader Profile". [1][2][3]

### 6.2 Stealth Assessment Telemetry and Data Privacy
Eldoria-V2's educational efficacy relies entirely on capturing gameplay metrics without violating privacy standards such as the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), which mandates strong data minimization. Google Firebase provides the architecture for entirely anonymous, session-based event tracking.

Firestore NoSQL document databases are utilized to log unstructured stealth assessment events. When a student completes the "Golem Programming" quest, Firebase instantly logs a secure event containing: Session_ID, Grade_Level_Profile, KUSP_Tag_ID, Time_To_Completion, Error_Count, and Final_Algorithm_Array. This raw telemetry is then aggregated to generate a non-PII (Personally Identifiable Information) exportable learning summary for parents and teachers, directly supporting the diagnostic literacy cycles mandated by the Glamorgan SDP. [1][2][3]

### 6.3 Performance Profiling and Optimization
Because the overarching product strategy emphasizes a "desktop + tablet/mobile first" approach, rigorous performance optimization is non-negotiable. The design guide establishes strict hardware limits for mobile targets: no more than 128 MB of resident 2D texture memory per scene, a maximum of 120 visible draw calls during battle sequences, and a cap of 6 simultaneously resident gameplay atlases. The Google Play Console's internal testing tracks and Android Vitals must be utilized continuously to profile the game's memory footprint and battery usage on mid-tier hardware, ensuring the high-fidelity pixel art and dynamic lighting remain performant without compromising the seamless traversal requirements of the game. [1][2][3]

## 7. Conclusion
The architectural design of Eldoria-V3 represents a fundamental paradigm shift away from superficial "edutainment." By rejecting overt, disruptive quizzes in favor of Evidence-Centered Design and rigorous stealth assessment, the game preserves the immersive, flow-inducing qualities of top-tier 2D RPGs while silently gathering highly granular, actionable data on student mastery. [1][2][3]

To effectively serve the students of Glamorgan School and the broader Calgary Board of Education, the game's mechanics have been engineered to deeply internalize the Traditional Learning Centre (TLC) philosophy. This is achieved by replacing standard RPG alignment with a virtues-based Character Education system centered on the 7 Habits, structuring all tutorials around the Gradual Release of Responsibility, and emphasizing structured, morphologically aware disciplinary literacy in all UI and dialogue systems, directly supporting the school's developmental goals. [1][2][3]

The integration of the newly updated Alberta Grade 2 and Grade 5 curricula ensures that the game's mechanics are the literal manifestation of the provincial learning outcomes. Whether a player is debugging a golem's movement algorithm to master Grade 2 Computer Science, calculating the aerodynamic lift required to glide across a canyon for Grade 5 Physics, or negotiating a trade treaty based on the Haudenosaunee Great Law of Peace for Grade 5 Social Studies, the learning remains experiential, consequential, and seamlessly woven into the heroic fantasy. By leveraging this exhaustive pedagogical framework alongside advanced telemetry and asset pipelines, Eldoria-V3 will deliver an unparalleled educational gaming experience.
